Project Senegal Secondary School — Building Opportunity in Djilakh
Location Djilakh, Thiès Region (Sindia Commune), Senegal
Competition Archstorming × Let's Build My School (LBMS)
Program 4 classrooms, admin wing, multipurpose hall, storage, restrooms — approx. 160 students
Budget €48,000 – €60,000
Construction Compressed earth blocks, bamboo structure, corrugated metal roofing

Design Concept

The design responds to Djilakh's educational crisis — a village of 4,000 inhabitants where the absence of a secondary school leads to high dropout rates. The proposal creates a modular campus of interconnected circular pavilions arranged around two large courtyards, preserving six existing trees at the heart of the site.

A radial structural system of bamboo posts and ring beams supports deeply overhanging roofs, enabling passive cooling through cross-ventilation and shaded circulation. Stabilised compressed earth blocks form the building envelope, providing thermal mass against extreme heat while remaining constructable by local workers without heavy machinery.

Colorful translucent panels in cobalt blue, amber yellow, and lime green punctuate the earth-toned facades, filtering light into classrooms and marking thresholds between learning and gathering spaces. The courtyard amphitheatre doubles as a multipurpose cultural venue — the school as social hub.
